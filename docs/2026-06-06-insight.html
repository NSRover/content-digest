When Did Trying Hard Become Lame? - Joe Santagato
gemini-flash (gemini-2.5-flash)
Guest

Joe Santagato is a content creator who discusses the trend of people trying to be nonchalant. He shares his perspective on the value of trying hard and being passionate about one's pursuits.

TLDR

The video argues that the current trend of nonchalance and irony, particularly online, stems from insecurity and a societal aversion to genuine enthusiasm, ultimately robbing individuals of the deep satisfaction and personal growth that comes from putting in hard work and earning success.

Key points
  • There's a prevalent trend, especially online, where people act nonchalant and speak in irony, avoiding genuine statements or showing true enthusiasm for fear of being perceived as "too keen" or "lame."
  • This nonchalant attitude often masks insecurity, as individuals are afraid to try hard and potentially fail, preferring to appear as if they weren't invested in the first place.
  • Actively trying hard and being willing to fail provides a significant advantage, as it allows for learning, growth, and the ability to move on from what doesn't work, rather than being stuck in inaction.
  • The greatest satisfaction comes from the internal validation of knowing you worked hard and earned something, rather than seeking external approval or making things easy for yourself.
  • Relying on tools like AI for creative pursuits can rob individuals of the valuable experience of putting in the effort, learning, and getting good at something through consistent practice, which is the true payoff.
Takeaway

To understand the world and make more autonomous choices, recognize that the widespread nonchalance and ironic detachment often seen in social interactions and online is frequently a manifestation of insecurity and a fear of failure. Instead of succumbing to this pressure, embrace genuine effort and enthusiasm. The true reward and personal growth come from the process of trying hard, even if it means failing, because "hard wins change you" and provide a deep sense of earned accomplishment that easy wins or external validation cannot.
