Raw Truths From The Brink Of Death – Ben Askren
gemini-flash (gemini-2.5-flash)
Guest

Ben Askren is a former Olympic wrestler and MMA champion who recently underwent a double lung transplant. He shares his experience of coming face-to-face with death and how it reshaped his perspective on life, priorities, and definition of success.

TLDR

After a near-fatal necrotizing pneumonia led to a double lung transplant, Ben Askren's experience reinforced his existing life philosophy of hard work, discipline, and living authentically, while also deepening his gratitude and commitment to meaningful pursuits.

Key points
  • Ben Askren's near-death experience began with what he thought was a back spasm, which turned out to be a severe staff infection that caused necrotizing pneumonia, essentially eating his lungs from the inside out.
  • He was unconscious for 37 days, during which he was airlifted, put on ECMO (a life support system with a low survival rate), and eventually received a double lung transplant.
  • Waking up with no memory of the preceding weeks, he was flabbergasted by his situation and the need for a transplant, having never smoked or been unhealthy.
  • His recovery was a long and arduous process, involving significant muscle loss, learning to walk and perform basic functions again, and dealing with complications like infections and chest tubes.
  • The experience reinforced his existing priorities, making him more patient and grateful, and led him to shed business investments he wasn't passionate about to focus more on coaching wrestling and his family.
  • He believes his athletic background, particularly the discipline and regimentation from wrestling, was crucial in his recovery, enabling him to consistently work towards getting better even on difficult days.
  • Despite the median life expectancy for lung transplant patients being six and a half years, Ben is determined to live at least 39 more years, aiming to be the longest-living double lung transplant recipient, and is open to future organ-building technologies.
Takeaway

Life can throw unexpected, life-altering challenges, and how one responds is largely shaped by existing mental fortitude and priorities. Cultivating discipline, perseverance, and a "white belt mentality" (always being open to learning and improvement) are crucial for navigating adversity and achieving long-term goals. Don't let external opinions or the fear of failure dictate your choices; instead, focus on what genuinely matters to you, take opportunities, and live life fully, as "the point of life is not to arrive safely at death."
