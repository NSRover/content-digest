The Secret Reason the US Dollar will NOT Die of Weakness
gemini-flash (gemini-2.5-flash)
TLDR

The US dollar system is structured such that almost every dollar is created as debt, making it resilient to weakness. The dollar cannot die from weakness because every move towards a weaker dollar creates more dollar debt, thereby strengthening the system. If the dollar system ever breaks, it will be due to strength, not weakness, and this endgame is further off than commonly believed.

Key points
  • The global monetary system is credit-based, meaning almost every dollar in existence began as someone's debt, loaned into existence by banks, sovereigns, or central banks. The only exceptions are physical currency, coins, and reserves held at the Federal Reserve.
  • Countries outside the US must manage two simultaneous carry trades: a global one in dollars and a local one in their own currency. These two trades are often in opposition, creating constant pressure on one side.
  • The US, however, only runs one carry trade, as its external obligations are denominated in the same currency it issues, giving it a significant structural advantage.
  • The Eurodollar market operates entirely outside the US base money perimeter and is leveraged end-to-end, meaning every Eurodollar deposit is a claim on a bank that holds claims on other banks.
  • The "dollar milkshake theory" provides the lens for understanding this system, suggesting that the dollar's resilience is often misunderstood, and its eventual demise, if it occurs, will be from strength, not weakness.
Takeaway

For a retail European investor, the video suggests that the US dollar's strength is structurally embedded and its demise is not imminent due to weakness. This implies that betting against the dollar based on perceived weakness might be a losing strategy in the long term. For a long-term investor, this reinforces the idea of dollar-denominated assets potentially maintaining their value or even appreciating due to the dollar's inherent strength. For a short-term trader, understanding this structural advantage of the dollar could inform strategies around currency pairs, potentially favoring dollar strength in certain market conditions, rather than anticipating its collapse.
