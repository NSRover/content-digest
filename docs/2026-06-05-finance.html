Key points
  • Long-term holders are investors who hold their coins for over 155 days, and their transfer behavior significantly influences market inflection points, including midterm to long-term tops and lows.
  • A surge in LTH transfer volume to exchanges often signals a potential change in market direction or a slowdown in price momentum.
  • The metric can be smoothed using moving averages (e.g., 7-day) to reduce noise and highlight significant trends.
  • By examining variations of this metric, such as LTH realized profit or loss to exchanges, investors can determine if large transfers are driven by profit-taking or loss realization, which is particularly relevant in different market regimes.
  • In a bear market, an increase in LTH loss realization to exchanges, especially when combined with market weakness, can indicate potential further price corrections. Conversely, a cooling down of loss realization can signal a resolution of supply from top holders, creating favorable conditions for a bull market.
