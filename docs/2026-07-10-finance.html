Key points

  • The rapid increase in Japanese government bond yields is a key catalyst, as Japanese investors repatriate capital from abroad, creating ripple effects and pushing up yields in other G7 bond markets.
  • Rising bond yields mean higher interest payments for governments, elevated mortgage rates, reduced business borrowing, and slower economic growth, as debt servicing crowds out productive spending.
  • The current situation is not a temporary blip but a fundamental questioning of confidence in government finances, central banks, and fiat currencies, with hard assets like precious metals potentially benefiting.
