The market is experiencing a historic momentum unwind and factor rotation out of crowded AI and technology trades into sectors like financials, industrials, and equal-weight S&P 500 stocks. This violent shift is being amplified by the reverse effects of leveraged ETFs, high single-stock volatility, and emerging fundamental cracks in the AI sector, such as cheap Chinese open-weight models and rising hyperscaler credit risks. Key points
  • The market is undergoing a severe momentum unwind, with Morgan Stanley reporting the selloff as the worst in 27 years. Capital is rotating out of AI infrastructure and moving into financials, industrials building data centers, and equal-weight S&P 500 stocks, reminiscent of the 2001 tech bubble rotation into smaller-cap stocks.
  • The massive growth of leveraged ETFs, which now account for approximately $60 billion in assets, has altered market structures. The gamma squeeze that previously drove massive inflows is now working in reverse, exacerbating market volatility during outflows.
  • The AI trade is facing fundamental cracks due to the emergence of cheap, highly capable Chinese open-weight models like Qwen 3, which offer comparable performance to frontier models at a fraction of the cost. This cheap substitution threatens the valuation of expensive frontier AI models and hyperscalers facing negative free cash flow and rising credit spreads.
  • The Federal Reserve's reliance on lagging, backward-looking data and forward guidance is criticized as a failure that increases market volatility. With forward inflation break-evens signaling low inflation, critics argue the Fed is being overly hawkish and slow to react to shifting economic realities.
  • Global carry trade risks are rising due to low rate and FX volatility, alongside policy shifts such as Japanese discussions on capital repatriation and actions by the Bank of Korea. This has triggered a stronger yen, a weaker dollar, and liquidations that have heavily impacted Korean retail traders.
  • The extreme concentration of the S&P 500 in the "Magnificent Seven" and semiconductor stocks makes the current market environment highly vulnerable and contagious compared to the 2000 tech bubble, though a cheapening of AI models could eventually spark a margin-driven revival for traditional value businesses.
  • Geopolitical tensions and physical disruptions, including Ukrainian attacks on Russian refining and logistics assets, are drawing down global oil inventories and creating multi-pronged pressures on supply. Concurrently, shifting housing policies in regions like New York and Canada are threatening property rights, driving a migration of businesses and families to states like Texas and Florida.
