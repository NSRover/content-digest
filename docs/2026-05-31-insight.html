The Real Problem With Online Dating
gemini-flash (gemini-2.5-flash)
TLDR

Online dating misdirects our efforts by suggesting the primary challenge in love is finding the "right" person, when the real work lies in learning to navigate relationships with imperfect individuals.

Key points
  • Online dating platforms promote the idea that the main obstacle to love is simply locating a suitable partner.
  • While finding a compatible candidate is necessary, it's often easier than people assume.
  • The true difficulty in relationships, which dating culture largely ignores, is learning how to coexist and resolve conflicts with another human being.
  • Dating culture encourages people to attribute relationship problems to being with the "wrong" person, rather than accepting that all partners will have issues.
  • This constant search for the "next shiny object" diverts effort from the essential work of building and maintaining a relationship with an "okay" candidate.
Takeaway

Instead of endlessly searching for a perfect partner, a more effective approach to relationships is to accept that everyone is problematic and focus on developing the skills to live and work through challenges with a reasonably compatible person. This reframes the problem from "finding the right person" to "doing the real work of getting on with another human being."
